Beyti Kebab

Description

Beyti Kebab is another famous kebab variety of Turkish cuisine that is basically made with grilled ground beef wrapped in phyllo dough sheets and topped with tomato sauce and served together with the yogurt. Source.

This tasteful kebab variety is named by Beyti Güler, the owner of the popular steakhouse restaurant called Beyti in Istanbul. Beyti Restaurant is one of the oldest steakhouses in Istanbul. Beyti Restaurant was opened in 1945 by Crimean immigrant Abdülmuttalip Güler and his son Beyti Güler as a small meatball restaurant with 4 tables and 16 chairs on the shore of Küçükçekmece Lake. In a few years period, the restaurant has become one of the most famous grilled steakhouses in Istanbul.

Ingredients

  • 800 gram Ground beef 80% lean and 20% fat
  • 1/3 cup Breadcrumbs
  • 1 Egg beaten
  • 1 Onion – medium size grated
  • 3 Cloves of garlic garlic-pressed
  • 2 teaspoon Red pepper flakes
  • 1 teaspoon Salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon Ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon Ground black pepper

For Wrap

  • 8 Thin flour tortillas 25-30cm in diameter each

For Tomato Sauce

  • 3 Garlic cloves finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes – medium size grated
  • 3 tbsp Olive oil
  • 1 tbsp Tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon Ground red pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon Salt

For Garlic Yogurt Mix

  • 350 gram Whole milk or strained yogurt
  • 2 Garlic cloves garlic pressed

For Garnishing

  • 1/2 Bunch flat-leaf parsley finely chopped

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, place the ground beef, breadcrumbs, grated onion, beaten egg, finely pressed garlic, salt, ground cumin, red pepper flakes, and ground black pepper. Combine all ingredients very well. Cover the bowl with stretch film and let the mixture rest and settle in the fridge for 30 minutes.
  2. Have a small bowl of water with a drizzle of olive oil to prevent the kofte mix from sticking to your hands while shaping the kofte rolls.
  3. Preheat the fan oven to 180°C/350°F.
  4. Place baking paper on a large baking pan and set aside. Dip your hand in the oily water and take about a small apple size of kofte mixture in your palm. Log roll it for about 12 cm in length and gently pat to flatten.
  5. Repeat this until all kofte mixture is used up. Place the kofte rolls side by side on the baking tray with about 3-4 cm space between them. You will have a total of 10 Beyti kofte rolls.
  6. Bake the kofte rolls in the preheated oven for about 25 minutes.
  7. Once the Beyti kofte rolls are baked, take the baking tray out of the oven and reduce the oven temperature to 160°C fan.
  8. Place the Beyti kofte rolls one by one on a large plate and set aside.
  9. Place and pat the thin flour tortillas over the Beyti kofte tray to soak up the kofte juice, then transfer them to a dry and clean surface.
  10. Place 2 (two) Beyti kofte rolls end to end on each thin dough tortilla to make one long roll. Roll them tightly into a log shape. Cut the log rolls into 4 or 5 equal pieces.
  11. Place a clean baking paper on the baking tray and arrange the Beyti rolls seam-side down on the tray. Repeat until all remaining kofte rolls are finished.
  12. Brush the top of the tortilla rolls with a little olive oil and bake them for about 5 minutes to make the tortilla rolls crispy.
  13. While the Beyti kofte rolls are baking, prepare the tomato sauce. Pour olive oil into a frying pan and add chopped garlic. Sauté for about one minute on medium heat. Add the grated tomatoes, tomato paste, and ground red pepper, then combine well. Simmer on medium to low heat for about 3 minutes.
  14. After preparing the tomato sauce, make the garlic yogurt mix. Combine the chopped garlic with the yogurt and set aside at room temperature.
  15. Arrange the Beyti kebab plate. Remove the baked rolls from the oven and place them in a circle shape on a serving plate. Place the garlic yogurt mix in the middle of the plate.
  16. Drizzle tomato sauce over the Beyti rolls and the garlic yogurt. Garnish with chopped parsley.
  17. Serve.
